# Channel Description — POV Life Simulation

## About

A "what if" life simulation channel that puts you inside extraordinary lives you'll never live.

Every episode narrates a real type of existence — the son of the US President, the heir to a billion-dollar empire, a child raised in witness protection — told entirely in second person. You are not watching someone else's story. You are living it for the next ten minutes.

Built on verified facts. Narrated with restraint. Animated with deliberate simplicity.

---

## What Makes This Different

- **World Registry:** Every episode world is built on real, verifiable facts — not fantasy. Secret Service protocols, White House room counts, presidential travel logistics. The life feels real because the details are.
- **Second Person Always:** No "he" or "she." Only "you." The stickman has no face because the face is yours.
- **Trilingual:** Each episode is produced simultaneously in English, Japanese, and Vietnamese — same emotional timing, adapted for each language's natural rhythm.
- **Earned Endings:** Nothing resolves easily. The close of each episode mirrors its opening — but carries the weight of everything that came between.

---

## Target Audience

- Age 16-35
- Viewers who find "what if" scenarios genuinely gripping
- People drawn to documentary, psychology, and identity-exploration content
- Curious about the hidden costs of extraordinary lives

---

## Content Format

- **Type:** Illustrated narrative — voice-over + stickman animation
- **Duration:** 8-15 minutes per episode
- **Visual style:** Minimalist stickman (faceless, anonymous) against richly detailed environments
- **Voice:** Calm, deliberate, second-person — English narration primary

---

## Visual Style

- **Character:** Stickman — round white circle head, thin black body, no facial features
- **Background:** Detailed, color-accurate, architecturally specific — Oval Office, Air Force One, suburban streets, foreign embassies
- **Contrast:** The simpler the character, the more immersive the world
- **Animation:** Slow, deliberate movement — no fast cuts, no rapid zooms

---

## Signature Elements

1. Opens with the moment of birth or arrival into the extraordinary world
2. "While others X, you Y." — the contrast line that anchors every episode
3. "You are [age]." — the time marker that moves the story forward
4. Short, isolated weight lines: *"This is not a home."* *"You never chose this."*
5. Callback close — opening image returns, carrying new meaning
