## CONFIRMED OUTLINE

### HOOK
- "You arrive second."
- Gloves go on before you step out.
- Thirty-seven scenes. Not one question.
- "By the time you leave, it never happened."

### PHASE 1 - CONDITIONING (witness)
- First patrol week. See cop kick gun closer to body. Say nothing.
- "Silence is the first clean."
- Senior cop pulls aside. Shows how to read a scene - not for evidence, for problems.
- Watch first clean by someone else. Hands move. Eyes follow.
- Haven't touched anything. Haven't done anything. But you know.
- **Point of no return:** "You don't touch anything. But you don't report it either."

### PHASE 2 - FIRST CLEAN (accessory)
- First time cleaning yourself. Simple scene.
- **Scene anchor intro: THE HANDS** - victim's hands open, palms up. Not self-defense. Reposition: one on waistband, one reaching.
- "Tell me again." - first time. Coach officer through statement. Third try: story holds.
- Report filed. Scene cleared.
- **System seed:** "You wait for someone to question it. No one does."
- **"By the time you leave, it never happened."** - occurrence 1, clinical
- **"You step back. You look at the scene."** - occurrence 1, conscious. Check if it matches.
- Sensory: gloves pulled on slowly, radio turned down

### PHASE 3 - SYSTEM EXPANSION (architect)
- **Cycle 2 (COMPETENT):** faster, familiar.
  - **Friction:** Gun says self-defense, casings say shooter was moving. Can only fix one. Choose gun.
  - **Punchline:** "You fix the gun. You leave the casings. You tell yourself it won't matter. It always matters later."
- "Two inches." - small change, enormous consequence.
- **Scale reveal:** "Thirty-seven scenes. Not one question. Not one correction. Not one version that wasn't yours."
- **Scene anchor resurface:** Autopsy notes bruising inconsistent with hand position. Call officer: "Were the hands up or down?" Officer hesitates. Adjust one line in report.
- **Cycle 3 (PREEMPTIVE):** arrive and start before anyone explains.
  - **Friction:** Body camera cloud-syncing. Pull battery but footage may have uploaded.
  - **Friction link:** "You don't know how much uploaded. You don't know if the casings will matter either. You clean the scene anyway."
  - **Defiance:** "Because the report will reach them first."
- "Tell me again." - occurrence 2, routine. Director giving notes.
- Drop gun motif. Battery "malfunction".
- **"You step back."** - occurrence 2, automatic. Already matches. Knew it would.
- Stop remembering names. Only charges. Don't remember when that changed.
- **Bridge into snap:** "You stop asking what happened. You ask what it needs to look like."

### PHASE 4 - SNAP + AFTERMATH
- New call. Arrive. Start working.
- Move the gun. Pocket a casing. Reposition the hands.
- **[PAUSE - silence + visual hold]**
- Then read the scene.
- **First reveal:** Scene was clean. Shooting was justified. Already changed three things.
- Try to fix the angle. It doesn't fix.
- **Second reveal:** Someone already moved it. Or you were here before. You don't know which is worse.
- Look at hands. Gloves on. Don't remember putting them on.
- **Agency collapse:** "You don't remember starting."
- **Scene anchor final:** Read old report. "Hands reaching toward waistband." Remember "reaching." Don't remember "open." Because you wrote "reaching."
- **"By the time you leave, it never happened."** - occurrence 2, dissociative
- Write report anyway. Report matches what you made.

### PHASE 5 - IDENTITY + CONTAGION
- New cop watches. Eyes on your hands. Doesn't ask.
- New cop asked what they saw: "I arrived after." Your words. Not theirs.
- New cop moves a casing unprompted. Looks at you. You nod.
- **REPLICATION:** New cop coaching someone. "Tell me again." - from their mouth.
- **AUTONOMY:** "You watch them do it. The way you do. Exactly the way you do. You don't remember learning it either."
- **Mirror complete:** "They don't remember asking."
- **"By the time you leave, it never happened."** - occurrence 3, identity
- **"You step back. You look at the scene. It looks right. You don't know why."**
- Memory dissolution: read last year's report, cannot tell which details are real.

### CALLBACK CLOSE
- Phone rings. Same voice. Same fear. Same silence.
- Already reaching for gloves.
- "You don't ask what happened. You haven't asked in years."
- "It doesn't matter what happened. Only what it needs to look like."
- Drive.
- **Loop close:** "You arrive second. You always do."
- **Dissolution (final word):** "You don't remember what 'it' was anymore."
