### Key Tensions
- **PROTECTION_VS_CONTROL (primary):** The badge means law enforcement - but the cop uses it as human resource management. The CI program designed to catch criminals creates a system of human ownership.
- **DUTY_VS_SELF:** The cop started with duty to remove drugs from streets. Duty became efficiency. Efficiency became inventory. The self that believed in justice now manages people like accounts.
- **POWER_VS_ISOLATION:** The power to decide who goes to prison and who becomes a tool isolates. The second phone IS the isolation - a parallel life no one knows about.

## CONFIRMED OUTLINE

### HOOK
- "You don't arrest people. You collect them."
- Fourteen on phone. Six on paper. Rest don't exist at all.
- Hook drop: not a rogue cop reveal - a system intro

### PHASE 1 - CONDITIONING (tool)
- Academy. CI program briefing. "Asset management."
- First patrol. First arrest - book normally. Sergeant shrugs: "One arrest."
- Senior detective pulls aside. Shows second phone. "This is how you get numbers."
- First time watching a flip. Booking room. Kid crying. Senior says three words. Kid nods.
- Weight: "Work this off." - first time hearing it. Sounds like a deal.

### PHASE 2 - FIRST FLIP (leverage)
- Your turn. Booking room.
- CI anchor intro: won't look at you. Hands shaking. Keeps asking if this clears it.
- "Work this off or do the time." You explain carefully. You mean it when you say "one buy."
- First deploy. No wire. No training. They buy. You wait in parking lot.
- They come back. Shaking. You file the bust. Their name not in it.
- Commendation. Sergeant nods. System confirms.
- "You own them." - first time. Clinical. Jargon. Professional distance.
- Check the phone. Not the department one. You look around first.

### PHASE 3 - THE TRAP (inventory)
- Second flip. Faster. Smaller charge. Bag of weed. Leverage is leverage.
- Third. Fourth. List grows.
- Time distortion: First week, you remember their names. Third week, you remember their charges. You don't remember when that changed.
- Off-book layer: department knows about six. Others live on the second phone only.
- CI anchor dependent: calls at 2AM. Not to report. To ask if safe.
- Brings names you didn't ask for. Trying to stay useful. Trying to stay alive.
- **RESISTANCE:** CI anchor hesitates. Longer this time. "I think I'm done." [pause] You slide the charge sheet back.
- **FALSE EXIT:** "When does this end?" - "After this one, you're clear." [pause] You already know that's not true.
- Loop cycle 3: "Work this off." No explanation. Three words. Next.
- You stop seeing faces. You see output.
- Check the phone before the department one. Stopped looking around.

### PHASE 4 - SNAP + AFTERMATH (disposable)
- Foreshadow: CI anchor calls at 2AM night before. You don't answer.
- New target. You know target carries.
- Deploy CI anchor. No wire. No warning.
- Phone rings. Not department phone. The other one.
- You wait for breathing.
- There is none.
- **AFTERSHOCK:** Line stays open. You don't hang up. [pause] You wait anyway.
- [silence]
- Delete thread. Remove number. Close file.
- "They were never yours."
- Cold calculation: "You still have thirteen."
- "You own them." - second time. Not metaphor anymore.

### PHASE 5 - IDENTITY + CONTAGION (ownership)
- New detective watches you flip someone. Takes notes.
- "Don't ask. Tell."
- New detective flips first CI alone. Uses your words. Your phrasing.
- **MIRROR:** They ask if this is how it's supposed to work. [pause] You hand them the phone.
- Next week, they don't ask anymore.
- You look at phone. Thirteen numbers. Remember charges. Not faces.
- "You own them." - third time. Identity. Permanent.
- Check pocket for phone. Phone gone. Hand still reaches.

### CALLBACK CLOSE
- Interrogation room. Someone new sits down. Same chair.
- Same charge. Same offer.
- You already know what they'll say.
- "You own them." You don't remember when that stopped being a figure of speech.
