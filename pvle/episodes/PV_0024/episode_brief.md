### Key Facts Used
1. You drive a rented sedan - never the same car twice, never your own
2. You receive coordinates by text message: a pickup time, a drop-off location, and a number that tells you how many hours you have
3. The person in the backseat has been told to trust you. They do not know your name. You do not know theirs.
4. You stop only at pre-approved locations - specific gas stations where no cameras face the pumps
5. Cold ischemia time for a kidney is 24 hours maximum - you have been told you have 6 to deliver the cooler
6. You carry two phones: one for the broker, one for directions. Neither has your real number.
7. You were a regular driver before - delivery, taxi, long-haul. Someone noticed you were reliable and quiet.
8. You earn between $1,000 and $3,000 per trip depending on distance - more than a month of legitimate driving
9. If you are stopped, the person in the backseat is your cousin visiting a doctor. The cooler contains food. That is the story.
10. You have never been inside the clinic. You stop at the gate. Someone comes out. The passenger gets out. You leave.

### Core Mechanic
**THE_QUIET_CARGO** - The transporter never touches the organ. Never enters the operating room. Never meets the buyer. The horror is not in what you carry. The horror is in what you choose not to ask.

### Cargo Duality
- Inbound: Living person in backseat - nervous, quiet, sometimes crying
- Outbound: Cooler on backseat - cold, silent, balanced on a towel
- Convergence: The moment both feel the same weight to you

### Personal Anchor
**THE_DAUGHTER** - Never named. Never described. She exists only through time references and comparison points. She is the unit of measurement for how far you have drifted.

### Storytelling Device
**THE_REARVIEW_MIRROR** - Same gesture, same angle, same glass - but the meaning changes as you change. The mirror progression IS the moral arc.
