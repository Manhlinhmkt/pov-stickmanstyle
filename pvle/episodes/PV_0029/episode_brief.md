### Key Tensions
- **PROTECTION_VS_CONTROL (primary):** The badge means law enforcement - but the Ghost uses it to decide who exists in the system. The database is the gate. The cop decides who passes through and who vanishes.
- **DUTY_VS_SELF:** The cop started with silence - seeing another cop walk past booking and saying nothing. Silence became convenience. Convenience became method. The self that believed in procedure now authors absence.
- **POWER_VS_ISOLATION:** The power to erase existence isolates. The Ghost cannot share what they do, cannot verify their own memory, cannot distinguish between people they erased and people who were never there. The isolation is not social - it is perceptual.

## CONFIRMED OUTLINE

### HOOK
- "You pull them over."
- "You put them in the car."
- "You don't book them."
- "Not because you forgot. Because you decided not to."
- "They were never here."

### PHASE 1 - CONDITIONING (witness)
- First week. See cop lead someone past booking desk. Doesn't come back.
- Ask senior: "Where does that door go?" - Answer is a look.
- "The waiting room." Everyone knows. No one says.
- Haven't participated. Haven't touched. But silent.
- **"Silence is the first disappearance."**

### PHASE 2 - FIRST ERASURE (convenience)
- 3AM. Too much paperwork. Turn left instead of right.
- **Scene anchor intro: THE ROOM** - door with no label, room with no camera.
- "Does anyone know you're here?" - No. Door opens.
- **Snap pre-payment #1:** Their phone rings 3 times during drive. Turn it off. Remove battery. Battery in pocket.
- Next morning: they're gone. No one asks.
- **FALSE SAFETY beat:** Friend calls precinct. "No one by that name was brought in." You hear it across the room. System works. You exhale. First hit.
- Check system 3 times. Not for them. For yourself.
- **"They were never here."** - occurrence 1, procedural
- **"You check the system. No record."** - occurrence 1, anxious/relief

### PHASE 3 - ATTENTION RESET
- **Mini-hook:** "The second time, it's not easier. It's clearer."
- "The second time, you're not testing it. You're using it."

### PHASE 3 ARC 1 - SELECTION AS GAME (control)
- Second time. You CHOOSE. Not because of paperwork. Because you can.
- Test boundaries: person with phone that keeps ringing. Person with a job.
- Person who answers "yes" to "does anyone know you're here?"
- **Surprise:** You BOOK this one. Normal process. The contrast = the reveal: you are CHOOSING who gets rights.

### PHASE 3 ARC 2 - THE CALLS (method)
- **SCENE 1 - Lawyer call:**
  - "The lawyer calls."
  - "You hear the sergeant."
  - "'No record.'"
  - "The client is in the room. Thirty feet."
- **SCENE 2 - Lawyer calls again:**
  - "The lawyer calls again."
  - "Same answer."
  - "The client stops asking for the phone."
- **Snap pre-payment #2:** Walk past room. Hear voice inside asking for a phone call. Recognize the voice. Don't stop.
- The number: "How many right now." Check it like checking the weather.
- **EARLY DISTORTION (light):** Walk past room. Hear something. Don't remember leaving anyone inside.

### PHASE 3 ARC 3 - ROOM > COP (infrastructure)
- **DISTORTION #1:** Check room before shift. Not empty. Should be empty.
- Don't remember putting them there.
- "Either you forgot. Or the room has another user now."
- **DISTORTION #2:** Count doesn't match. One more than you remember. Recount. Same number.
- The room has exceeded the cop. Infrastructure is alive.
- **Bridge into snap:** "You stop choosing carefully. You start choosing conveniently."

### PHASE 4 - SNAP + AFTERMATH
- **Pre-payment #3:** Person in room stops asking about phone. Asks when they can leave. You don't answer.
- Night. Standing near booking desk. Precinct phone rings.
- **Identity confirmation:** "You know who the child is calling about."
- **EVENT:** Child's voice. Looking for parent.
- "No one by that name was brought in tonight."
- Child asks again. Same answer.
- **MICRO-HESITATION:** Almost step forward. Almost. Foot moves. Weight shifts. Then stops. Stepping forward = unwinding every absence.
- 30 feet. One wall. Door with no label. Child hangs up. Dial tone.
- **"They were never here."** - occurrence 2, possessive (AFTER snap)
- **Cold calculation (minimal):**
  - "The system works."
  - "The database is empty."
  - Silence.

### PHASE 5 - IDENTITY + CONTAGION
- New cop sees you turn left instead of right. Doesn't ask.
- "What person?" - Your words. Their mouth.
- New cop holds door to room. Doesn't look inside.
- **REPLICATION:** New cop leads someone to room alone.
- **MIRROR:** Hear new cop ask "Does anyone know you're here?"
- "You don't remember teaching them."
- "You don't remember learning it either."
- **SYMMETRY:** "They don't ask what's in the room. You didn't either."
- **"They were never here."** - occurrence 3, identity
- **"You check the system. No record."** - occurrence 3, confusion

### CALLBACK CLOSE
- **PAIN CALLBACK + ACTIVE DELETION:**
  - "The phone."
  - "It rang three times."
  - [pause]
  - "You delete it."
- "Names create records. Records create people."
- Someone new in the car. Same route. Same door. Same room.
- Don't ask their name.
- **Loop close:** "They were never here."
- **Dissolution (final word):** "You don't remember if they were."
