### Key Tensions
- **PROTECTION_VS_CONTROL (primary):** The badge means protection - but the cop discovers the weapon means control. The system designed to protect enables addiction to deciding who is dangerous.

- **POWER_VS_ISOLATION:** The power to decide who gets shot isolates. The partner who sees what really happened becomes the biggest threat. The rookie who mirrors you becomes the proof you can never undo it.

## CONFIRMED OUTLINE

### HOOK
- The call comes in. You haven't arrived yet. You already know who it is.
- Not because you know them.
- "You don't protect people. You sort them."

### PHASE 1 - CONDITIONING (fear)
- Training room. Instructor standing before class.
- "I feared for my life." - taught like a formula. BEFORE teaching aim.
- First patrol. Weapon is heavy on your hip. Hope you never use it.
- Hand touches holster. Notice. Pull back.

### PHASE 2 - FIRST HIGH (control)
- First draw. Unarmed. They freeze.
- Everything stops. Except you. You decide what happens next.
- You wait for the fear. It doesn't come.
- "Show your hands. Get on the ground." Slow. Deliberate. Pause between.
- "You reach before you think." - first time, notice, pull hand back.
- Camera blinks red. You look at it.

### PHASE 3 - ESCALATION (habit)
- Draw. They stop. Everything stops. Except you. Don't fire.
- Draw faster. They haven't stopped yet. Already shouting. Everything stops. Except you.
- Don't wait for them to stop.
- Don't wait for them to understand.
- "Hands up. On the ground. Don't move." Faster. Overlapping.
- Fire. [silence] Board clear. Forty minutes for a life.
- "Furtive movement." Already wrote that before. Don't remember when.
- Fire again. Didn't need to. Report says otherwise.
- Shooting count highest in precinct. Sergeant calls you "proactive."
- Except you.
- "I feared for my life." Automatic. No thought.
- Camera click. Don't look.

### PHASE 4 - SNAP MOMENT (peak)
- "Show your ID."
- They reach for wallet.
- "Hands-"
- Fire.
- [silence]
- "Show your ID."
- It was already in their hand.
- Don't remember deciding to fire.
- [silence]
- Rookie hesitates. You don't. Rookie fires after you.
- Rookie looks at you. The way you used to look at someone else.

### PHASE 5 - ERASURE (identity)
- Teach rookie the five words.
- "I feared for my life." Rookie's voice. Sounds wrong.
- Same phrase. Different person. The system replicates.
- Look at the report. Recognize the words. Not the person.
- ID photo in the corner of the file. Don't remember seeing that face.
- Read the report again. One detail is wrong. Don't fix it.
- Remember writing it. Don't remember why.
- [silence]
- "I feared for my life." Don't remember if you did.

### CALLBACK CLOSE
- The call comes in. Same format. Different person.
- Hand already on holster before dispatcher finishes.
- Hand was already there. You didn't place it.
