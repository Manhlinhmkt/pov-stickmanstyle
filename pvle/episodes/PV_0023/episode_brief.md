### Key Facts Used
1. You place the stethoscope the same way every time. Left hand steadies. Right hand listens. The heart does not know who is asking. (ALL PHASES: the diagnostic loop as recurring motif)
2. You write "compatible" on the form. You do not write what compatible means for the person standing in front of you. (PHASE_CONFLICT, PHASE_RESOLUTION: semantic duality)
3. You run trafficking samples through the hospital lab under false names - between the 10am routine bloodwork and the 11am cholesterol check. (PHASE_CONFLICT: system integration)
4. The broker calls you "Doctor." Not your name. Never your name. You prefer it that way. (PHASE_EARLY, PHASE_CONFLICT: identity fragmentation)
5. You see every donor face-to-face. You are the only person in the chain who touches them before the surgeon does. (PHASE_CONFLICT, PHASE_CRISIS: intimacy of the medical exam)
6. When a donor asks "Is this safe?" you answer with the same sentence every time. You have practiced it. (PHASE_CONFLICT: rehearsed clinical response)
7. You rejected one once. Kidney function below threshold. The broker thanked you. A week later, someone else cleared them. (PHASE_CRISIS: the uselessness of refusal)
8. Every illegal transplant requires a medical evaluation first - blood type, tissue matching, kidney function, infectious disease screening - without this step, the organ is worthless (ALL: the doctor as bottleneck)
9. Cross-match testing determines if a donor kidney will be rejected by the recipient - the doctor who runs this test holds the power to approve or reject every transaction (PHASE_CONFLICT: gatekeeper power)
10. A single compatibility evaluation earns $3,000-$8,000 - more than a month of legitimate salary in many countries where trafficking networks operate (PHASE_EARLY: economic trap)
11. The evaluating doctor never enters the operating room - results go to the broker, who sends the donor to the surgeon - the chain is deliberately fragmented (ALL: deliberate separation)
12. The same medical skills used to protect patients in the morning are used to select victims in the evening - the knowledge does not change, only the purpose (PHASE_RESOLUTION: convergence)

### Key Locations
- LOC_LEGITIMATE_CLINIC: Where you practice real medicine during daylight - the same stethoscope, the same lab coat, the same hands
- LOC_EVENING_EXAM_ROOM: Back office or rented room where donors are brought for evaluation - same medical equipment, different purpose
- LOC_LAB: Where blood samples are processed under false names - your handwriting on the requisition form is the only thing that connects you
- LOC_PHONE_CALL: Where you deliver results to the broker in coded language - "compatible" means the donor goes to the surgeon
- LOC_MORNING_AFTER: Your legitimate clinic the next day - seeing real patients with the same hands that signed the clearance

### Key Tensions
- **DUTY_VS_SELF**: You took the Hippocratic oath. You also took the debt. The medical system trained you to heal. The economic system forced you to choose between healing and surviving. The same diagnostic skill serves both purposes - the duty splits in half and both halves feel like duty.
- **NORMAL_DESIRE_VS_IMPOSSIBLE_REALITY**: You want to practice medicine. You want to pay your debts. You want to be a good doctor. The only way to do all three runs through a back office with no name on the door and a broker who calls you "Doctor" but never asks your name.
- **IDENTITY_VS_LEGACY**: You trained for years. Your hands know what they were made for. But the legacy of your training is not what you imagined - your expertise is the bottleneck of a trafficking network. The skills are your pride and your chain simultaneously.
