- **Domain:** MILITARY_INTELLIGENCE

### Key Facts Used

1. Mossad recruits primarily from elite IDF units - candidates undergo multi-stage psychological screening, simulations under assumed identities, and stress tests lasting months before acceptance - used in PHASE_EARLY (recruitment)
2. Training at the Midrasha academy near Herzliya lasts up to two years - covering surveillance detection, agent recruitment, cover identity management, and operating in hostile environments - used in PHASE_EARLY (training)
3. A katsa (case officer) is the Mossad's core field operative - responsible for finding, recruiting, and running human sources (agents) in foreign countries under deep cover identities - used in PHASE_CONFLICT (operational life)
4. The Mossad relies on a global network of sayanim - Jewish diaspora volunteers who provide logistical support (vehicles, safe houses, medical care) to operatives without being on payroll - used in PHASE_CONFLICT (operational network)
5. Operatives live under cover identities for years abroad - often as businessmen, students, or travelers - managing a psychological split between their real and operational personas that former agents describe as partial schizophrenia - used in PHASE_CONFLICT and PHASE_CRISIS
6. Families of agents live in a house of silence - spouses often know only that their partner works for a vague government entity, children grow up confused by unexplained absences and strict security rules - used in PHASE_CONFLICT and PHASE_RESOLUTION
7. HUMINT work requires systematic manipulation and deception of recruited sources - building genuine trust with people you intend to use, crossing moral boundaries that are unacceptable in civilian life - used in PHASE_CRISIS (moral injury, self-disgust)
8. Former agents report extreme difficulty reintegrating into civilian life - the psychological effects of years of deception, the feeling of neglect by the state after service, and the lifelong obligation to keep secrets - used in PHASE_RESOLUTION

### Key Locations

- **LOC_MIDRASHA** - Mossad training academy near Herzliya - where recruits learn to become someone else
- **LOC_COVER_CITY** - A European capital where the operative lives under a fabricated identity for years - cafe meetings, surveillance routes, dead drops
- **LOC_SAFE_HOUSE** - A rented apartment provided by a sayan - no name on the lease, no trace, temporary home between operations
- **LOC_HOME_ISRAEL** - A modest apartment in a quiet Israeli suburb - where the family waits without knowing when or why
- **LOC_HEADQUARTERS** - The nondescript building in Tel Aviv where debriefings happen and new missions begin

### Key Tensions

- **DUTY_VS_SELF** - You serve your country by becoming someone else. You build genuine trust with people you will betray. Every relationship is a weapon - and you are the one who pulls the trigger.
- **IDENTITY_VS_LEGACY** - After years of cover identities, you forget which version of yourself is real. Your children grow up with a father who is never fully present and never fully honest.
- **NORMAL_DESIRE_VS_IMPOSSIBLE_REALITY** - You want a normal family, a normal friendship. But every word you speak to anyone outside the agency is filtered, calculated, incomplete.
- **PROTECTION_VS_CONTROL** - You are told you protect your nation. But what you do to individuals who trust you feels less like protection and more like violation.

## CONFIRMED OUTLINE

### HOOK
- Handshake in a European cafe - warm smile, eye contact, friendly conversation
- "They trust you. That is how you win."
- The face across - you know their real name, they do not know yours
- "You have never told anyone who you are. Not even the person sitting next to you every day."

### PHASE_EARLY: Recruitment + Training
- Elite IDF unit - noticed by someone you never see
- Mysterious phone call - interview, no organization name given
- Months of psychological screening - assumed identities, stress simulations
- Midrasha, Herzliya - two years of learning to be someone else
- Tradecraft: surveillance detection, source recruitment, cover management
- Final exercise: three weeks in a foreign city with a fake identity - no one knows who you are
- "You graduate. No diploma. No photo. Nothing proves you were ever here."

### PHASE_CONFLICT: Operating Under Cover
- **Tonal direction: self-disgust - not just sadness, but moral revulsion at turning trust into a weapon**
- Deployed to a European capital - new identity: businessman, apartment, regular cafe
- First mission: approach a target - scientist, diplomat, or businessman with access
- Build relationship slowly - dinners, coffee, birthdays, personal sharing
- Target starts to trust you - tells you about family, children, fears
- You turn every private story they share into an intelligence report - and you know it is wrong
- Phone call home to Israel - "Work is fine" - daughter asks when Abba is coming home
- Sayanim network - strangers give you cars, rooms, documents - no questions asked
- "You spend more hours with strangers than with your family. And the strangers think they know you."

### PHASE_CRISIS: The Order
- Target trusts you completely - invites you to their home for dinner with their family
- Their daughter is the same age as yours - running around the table laughing
- Phone buzzes mid-dinner - code: "Activate"
- You keep smiling, keep talking - no one notices
- **Deliberate choice: you could refuse. You could stand up, tell the truth, warn them. No one is holding a gun to your head. But you do not refuse. You choose to continue.**
- Extract final intelligence then burn the relationship
- You watch them pour wine for you - they do not know this dinner is the last
- Next morning the apartment is empty. The phone number no longer exists.
- You read the news 2 weeks later - they were arrested, or disappeared, or worse
- "They never knew who you were. That is the worst part."

### PHASE_RESOLUTION: The Cost of Safety
- Next mission, then the next - you stop counting
- Back in Israel between missions - daughter does not recognize you, runs to mom instead
- Wife says "I do not know who you are anymore" - you do not know either
- Leave Mossad - no retirement ceremony, no medal, no one says thank you
- Sitting in a Tel Aviv cafe - someone calls your name, you flinch - which name?
- See a therapist - "What trauma have you experienced?" - you are not allowed to answer
- Google the old name of your target - their social media is still there, family photos, smiles
- "You are safe. You were always safe. But you do not know who you are anymore."

### CALLBACK_CLOSE
- Another cafe. Another person sitting across from you. But this time there is no mission.
- Your hands still follow habit - scanning exits, counting people, memorizing faces
- "You lived two lives. And now you do not know which one was real."
