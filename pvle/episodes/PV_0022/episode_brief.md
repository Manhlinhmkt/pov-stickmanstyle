### Key Facts Used
1. You don't go to the ones who still have options. You wait until they stop arguing with themselves. (PHASE_CRISIS: recruitment instinct - knowing when to approach)
2. You lift your shirt and say: look, I am fine. They look at the scar. They stop asking questions. (PHASE_CONFLICT: the physical proof that completes the sentence)
3. You never say 'sell' or 'organ.' You say 'help.' You say 'opportunity.' You say 'just this once.' (PHASE_CONFLICT: language sanitization)
4. You never push. You wait. You let them say yes to themselves. You just stay quiet. (PHASE_CRISIS: silence as weapon)
5. You pause before saying the number. That pause matters more than the number itself. (PHASE_CRISIS: micro-behavior of manipulation)
6. Someone says no. You don't try again. You wait. They come back three days later. Everyone comes back. You know this because you came back too. (PHASE_EARLY + PHASE_RESOLUTION: the cycle that proves itself)
7. You earn $500-$1,000 per referral - a fraction of the broker's cut, but steady income for a body that can no longer do heavy labor (PHASE_CONFLICT: economic logic of becoming a recruiter)
8. Former sellers are recruited as recruiters specifically because they carry the scar - living proof that the procedure is survivable (PHASE_CONFLICT: why the system needs you specifically)
9. After enough repetitions, recruiters stop remembering which parts of the sentence are true and which parts they added to make it work (PHASE_CRISIS: identity erosion through language)
10. The line between the recruiter's own story and the recruitment script dissolves over time - they begin using the same persuasion patterns outside of work, with family, with themselves (PHASE_RESOLUTION: language contamination)

### Key Locations
- LOC_KIDNEY_VILLAGE: Where half the adults carry the same scar and you know exactly which doors to knock on
- LOC_TEA_STALL: Two cups, a low voice, and a number written on the back of a receipt
- LOC_TARGET_HOME: The kitchen table where you sit across from someone who trusts you and say the sentence you were taught
- LOC_BUS_STOP: Where you put someone on the overnight bus to the city - the last time they look at you before they go
- LOC_BROKER_CHECKPOINT: Roadside meeting where you hand over the referral and collect your envelope - the broker never remembers your name

### Key Tensions
- **DUTY_VS_SELF**: Your body can no longer work. Your family still needs to eat. The broker offers a way to earn without lifting - but it costs someone else what it cost you. The duty is to your family. The self is the person who remembers what it felt like.
- **NORMAL_DESIRE_VS_IMPOSSIBLE_REALITY**: You want steady income. You want to be useful again. The only path runs through other people's bodies - and the sentence you learned is the tool that opens that path.
- **POWER_VS_ISOLATION**: The more skilled you become at the sentence, the more respected you are in the village for your connections - and the more avoided you are for what those connections mean. Competence creates distance.

## EPISODE NOTES
- **Duration**: 15 min target, 1755-2340 word budget
- **Beat count**: estimated 22-28 beats (EXTENDED profile)
- **Veil enforcement**: N/A (GENERIC_ARCHETYPE - no named individuals to veil)
- **Emotional arc**: TENSION (hook - the sentence revealed as weapon) -> DREAD (deepening desperation + becoming a victim) -> TENSION (learning the sentence, first use) -> DREAD (escalation into competence) -> CLARITY (identity erosion complete) -> BITTERSWEET (the sentence lives without you) -> DEEP_CONNECTION (callback - you don't need to believe anymore)
- **Callback structure**: Hook opens with the signature sentence "You won't miss it. Not in the way you think." + transfer reveal; Callback closes with same sentence + "Ban khong can tin nua. Ho van tin."
- **Core mechanic**: THE_SENTENCE_PATTERN - the narrative is built around a recruitment sentence that follows a 3-part structure: problem_reduction -> relief_framing -> normalization. The sentence evolves across phases: INHERITED (EARLY) -> FULL (CONFLICT) -> SHORTENED (CRISIS) -> FRAGMENT/SILENCE (RESOLUTION). The sentence IS the plot.
- **Identity erosion arc**: The recruiter loses not their body (that happened in EARLY) but the boundary between themselves and the system. Tracked through 6 stages: INHERITED -> LEARNING -> PERFORMING -> IMPROVING -> MERGING -> DISSOLVED. By RESOLUTION, the sentence pattern has infected their daily language - they use it with family, with strangers, with themselves.
- **Sentence evolution (visual+narrative)**: Stage 0 INHERITED: someone said it to you (EARLY). Stage 1 FULL: you rehearse it alone, deliver full pitch (CONFLICT). Stage 2 SHORTENED: you say less, they say yes faster (CRISIS). Stage 3 FRAGMENT: just "how much do you need?" (late CRISIS). Stage 4 SILENCE: you hear someone else say it. It sounds right. (RESOLUTION).
- **Micro-behaviors (VO texture)**: The pause before the number. The silence after the pitch. Letting them say yes to themselves. Looking away when they cry - not shame, patience. Pouring the second cup before they ask. These behaviors appear in CONFLICT and CRISIS to create visceral realism.
- **Failure mechanics**: Not every target says yes immediately. The recruiter knows this. "Someone says no. You wait. They come back." This pattern appears in EARLY (subject's own cycle) and mirrors in RESOLUTION (subject watching others cycle). The power is patience, not persuasion.
- **Self-betrayal escalation**: CONFLICT: you use the sentence on a neighbor. CRISIS: you recruit someone younger than you were, who says "no" exactly like you did. RESOLUTION: you use the sentence pattern on your own family - not for recruitment, just to get what you want. The contamination is complete.
- **Ending logic**: No guilt. No redemption. No punishment. The ending lands on competence without belief: "You don't need to believe anymore. They still believe." This is colder than regret - it is the moment the recruiter realizes belief was never the point.
- **Series position**: Fifth perspective in organ trafficking series (PV_0015: Broker, PV_0016: Surgeon, PV_0017: Buyer, PV_0021: Donor, PV_0022: Recruiter). The bridge between victim and system - the person who proves the machine is self-replicating. POV sits at the exact point where empathy becomes a weapon.
- **Differentiation from PV_0021 (Donor)**: Donor = body as price (physical loss). Recruiter = language as weapon (identity loss). Donor's horror is what was taken. Recruiter's horror is what was learned. No overlapping scenes - PV_0021 ends where PV_0022 begins (post-surgery, body broken, system approaches with a new offer).
- **Platform safety**: No graphic surgical content. No body horror. No poverty porn or pity framing. No cool villain or con artist framing. No redemption arc. Subject framed as both victim and perpetrator simultaneously - corrupted empathy, not evil. Horror emerges from language and repetition, not violence. Ending is systemic contamination, not moralization.
- **Forbidden**: Graphic surgical imagery. Cool manipulator framing. Pure villain without humanity. Redemption arc or heroic whistleblower ending. Poverty porn. Moralizing ending. Documentary-style CTA. The word "sell" or "organ" in the recruitment scenes (use "help," "opportunity," "just this once").
